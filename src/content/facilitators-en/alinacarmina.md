For as long as I can remember, I have been deeply connected to the world around me, more sensitive to the environment I live in. I discovered that emotions and psycho-emotional states can somatize and manifest as various physical ailments, and often doctors could not identify the true causes of these problems. Over time I went through a carousel of experiences that affected me on multiple levels. That is how my healing journey began — when I decided to take responsibility for my own recovery and transformation.

The first moment of realization came fairly early, when I started with competitive dance and movement. My inner states began to calm as I trained in performance sport, which I practiced for nearly eight years. After a long break, when some issues returned, I understood how important movement is for me.

Over time I explored many forms of dance and movement (some of which I later taught) — from Latin social dance, contemporary dance, various hip‑hop styles, Axis Syllabus, contact improvisation, dance therapy, to movement disciplines such as calisthenics, natural movement, play‑fight, pilates, and tae‑bo. At the same time I discovered the transformative power of practices like yoga, qigong, Zen, Osho meditations, plant‑based/shamanic approaches, and reiki. I gradually understood how the body’s flexibility, tone and freedom of movement are tightly linked to the flexibility, tone and freedom of thought, moods and emotions.

These personal searches led me through many alternative medicine and complementary therapy methods, studied and applied on myself: massage therapy, sound healing and music, breathing techniques and meditation, dance therapy, herbal medicine, integrative nutrition and naturopathy, aromatherapy, Ayurveda, the connections between illnesses and psycho‑emotional states, and other related approaches.

In 2011 I began practicing manual therapy, and that passion set me on a path of continuous learning. I studied reflexology, somatic and relaxation massage, lymphatic and anticellulite massage, and completed a technician/therapist masseur course. In 2013 I discovered MMT (Mixed Manual Therapy), a complex therapeutic method — a mix of manual techniques drawn from various styles: Thai therapeutic massage, Yumeiho, Russian massage, shiatsu. After three years of intensive study I began applying these techniques at the Varuna Foundation, and since then I have continued to explore practices such as fascial and deep tissue massage, visceral abdominal massage (Chi Nei Tsang), acupressure point therapy, myofascial meridians and fascial adjustments, elements of osteopathy and craniosacral work, and ongoing exploration.

I started studying yoga in 2010 and practicing asana from 2013; later I became certified as an instructor at a Yoga Alliance–registered school. Through yoga practice many changes happened in me, both physically and on other levels — inside and out. The most constant feeling is that calm presence, a state of being beyond being someone, a movement toward harmony and well‑being.

All of this helped me know myself better, to look at myself honestly, to accept myself as I can, and to change where and how I can. They taught me gentleness toward myself and others, opened and shifted my perception, and filled me with wonder at how complex and interconnected everything — including the human being — truly is.

Postural Alignment came as a perfect complement to what I had learned so far. I understood again that postural reeducation is not only about the body but also about inner retraining. Now I know that when body, mind and heart are in harmony, profound change is possible.
